More Work, Less Energy

Being a hard worker is a good thing. At least, we think it is. And don’t get me wrong, busting one’s ass is sometimes necessary and completely unavoidable if you want to do anything worthwhile, whether it’s building a company, paying your bills, or raising your kids.

But I’ve started to wonder about all the times when hard work was, well, unnecessary. Or at least inefficient. And I’ve started to believe that’s the case a lot more often than we think.

I once wrote an if-else statement that was like 25 lines of code. (this was during my first two months of bootcamp, mind you) and it took forever. All the typing, copy/pasting, and carefully organizing my data ended up delivering me the result I was hoping for (the app worked!) but it was horribly, embarrassingly, inefficient. I could have done the same thing with about 4 line of code instead.

I also love tracking the hours I spend working on coding as a measure of how productive I’m being, or how “hard” I’m working. But if I had written that if-statement the efficient way, those hours I was so proud of logging simply wouldn’t have been logged. Does that mean I would’ve been a less hard worker? Well, yes. But it also means, in that case, I would have been a better coder.

Anyway, my thought for now is, there’s an important caveat to hard work that I think we should talk about more. It’s that it doesn’t mean much if the work is being done inefficiently. Work smart, not hard sums it up nicely. I made that up, by the way.
